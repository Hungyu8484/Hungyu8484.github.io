Auger Electron Spectrometer Research

NSRRC Research Group | Exploring Material Analysis through Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES)

This project was conducted at the National Synchrotron Radiation Research Center (NSRRC), Taiwan's leading facility for advanced science research utilizing synchrotron radiation. I was selected as a high school research intern after an application process, joining a team of four classmates under the supervision of NSRRC scientists.

Since my sophomore year of high school, I have spent every Tuesday afternoon at the center, conducting experiments and data analysis focused on improving Auger Electron Diffraction (AED) methods for material characterization. Throughout this ongoing project, I have presented our research project in multiple academic and outreach settings: including the school science fair and exchange programs in Japan. The research remains in progress, and I plan to continue contributing to the project in the coming year.
