Independent Linguistic Research

The Impact of Non-Linear Language on Cognitive Efficiency in Physics Reasoning:
A Comparison Between Circular Graphical Language and Linear Text
National Linguistic Science Fair 2nd Place

This is an independent linguistic research project I developed during the summer of 2025. I explored how non-linear visual languages can enhance cognitive efficiency in physics reasoning by comparing circular graphical representations and linear text. The project was presented at the Taiwan Linguistics Science Fair, where it earned Second Place nationwide among all high school finalists. Through this experience, I learned to merge linguistic theory with cognitive science and STEM education.
